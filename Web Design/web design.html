Why Wong Kar-wai

Introduction.

Wong Kar-wai, born on July 17, 1958 in Shanghai, China, is a famous Chinese film and television director, screenwriter, and producer.

Wong Kar-Wai is good at the unique aesthetics of light and shadow. He has won the Hong Kong Film Awards and the European Film Awards many times, and has won many major international film awards, including the Cesar Award, the New York Film Critics Circle Award, and the National Society of Film Critics Award. He has competed for the Palme d'Or, the Cannes Film Festival's highest honor, four times and is the first Chinese director to win the Cannes Film Festival's Best Director Award.

The best director in China?

Wong Kar-Wai's films have a strong personal style. He likes to use slow rhythms and appropriate music to bring out the atmosphere of the film. His photography techniques are also very unique. He often collaborated with Du Kefeng and Zhang Shuping, and was called the Iron Triangle by the outside world. Zhang and Du also made Wong Kar-wai's films more aesthetically pleasing . When Doyle shot Wong Kar-Wai's films, his photography techniques were also very unique, and his "handheld" and "half-covered" techniques became their trademarks.

Wong Kar-Wai loves to use actors with strong personal style, reputation for acting skills and excellent professionalism in his movies, such as Tony Leung, Leslie Cheung, Maggie Cheung, Jacky Cheung, Carina Lau, Faye Wong, etc. who are all frequent guests in his works;

Most of the plays directed by Wong Kar-Wai do not have pre-scripts, and the actors are only given lines on the day of filming. This means that the actors will not know the content of the play before watching the finished film. Wong Kar-wai's directing style has enabled many actors to give excellent performances in his works. Many actors also said that their acting skills have improved after filming Wong Kar-wai's movies.

Wong Kar-wai's major achievements include

Best Director Award at the 50th Cannes International Film Festival
Three Hong Kong Film Awards Best Director Award
The 28th Taiwan Film Golden Horse Awards Best Director Award
The 33rd Hong Kong Film Awards Best Screenplay Award
Best Director Award at the 4th Beijing International Film Festival
The 8th Asian Film Awards Best Director Award
2019 Hong Kong Film Writers Guild "Honorary Award"
